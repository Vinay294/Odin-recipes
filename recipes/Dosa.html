South Indian Dosa

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that is enjoyed throughout India and the world. It is a thin, crispy crepe-like dish made from a fermented batter of rice and lentils. Here is a detailed recipe for making dosa South Indian style

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup Parboiled Rice (idli-dosa rice)
  • 3/4 cup Regular Rice
  • 1/2 cup Whole Urad Dal (without skin) or Split Urad Dal (black lentils)
  • 1/4 teaspoon Fenugreek Seeds (methi dana)
  • 1/2 tablespoon Chana Dal (gram lentils), optional
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil, for shallow frying

Directions

  1. Soak the rice and urad dal separately for at least 6 hours, along with fenugreek seeds in the same container as urad dal.
  2. Drain the water from the soaked urad dal and grind it in a wet grinder or mixer grinder until it becomes a smooth and fluffy batter. This process takes around 10-15 minutes. Add little water as required to make it into a smooth batter.
  3. Take the urad dal batter in a bowl and keep it aside.
  4. Now, grind the soaked rice into a smooth batter. It should have a slightly coarse texture. Add water as required to make it into a smooth batter.
  5. Mix the rice batter with the urad dal batter, add salt to taste and mix well. The consistency of the batter should be slightly thick, like pancake batter.
  6. Cover the batter with a lid and allow it to ferment overnight or for at least 8 hours in a warm place. The batter should double in size and have a sour smell. If it is not fermenting, you can keep it in a warm place or place it in an oven with the light on for a few hours to aid the fermentation.
  7. After fermentation, give the batter a good mix.
  8. Heat a dosa tawa or non-stick pan over medium-high heat.
  9. Once the tawa is hot, sprinkle some water on it. If the water sizzles and evaporates immediately, the tawa is ready for use.
  10. Using a ladle, pour a ladle full of batter on the center of the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to make a thin crepe-like layer. You can make it as thin or thick as you like.
  11. Drizzle some oil around the edges and center of the dosa.
  12. Cook the dosa on medium-high heat for 1-2 minutes or until the bottom turns golden brown and crisp.
  13. Flip the dosa using a spatula and cook the other side for 30 seconds to a minute or until it is cooked and crispy.
  14. Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar or any other side dish of your choice.
