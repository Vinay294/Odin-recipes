Sarva Pindi

Description

Sarva Pindi is a popular savory dish from Telangana, India. It is also known as "Ginnappa" in some parts of the state. It is a type of flatbread made with rice flour, chana dal, onions, green chilies, and spices. Here's how to make it

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of rice flour
  • 1/2 cup of chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 3-4 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup of fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  • 1 tsp of cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp of sesame seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil for frying

Directions

  1. Soak chana dal for 3-4 hours and grind it into a coarse paste:
    Rinse 1/2 cup of chana dal (split Bengal gram) and soak it in water for about 3-4 hours. Drain the soaked chana dal and grind it into a coarse paste using a mixer grinder or food processor. Do not add water while grinding.
  2. Mix rice flour, chana dal paste, onions, green chilies, coriander leaves, cumin seeds, sesame seeds, and salt to taste in a large mixing bowl:
    In a large mixing bowl, add 2 cups of rice flour, the ground chana dal paste, 1 finely chopped onion, 3-4 finely chopped green chilies, 1/4 cup of chopped fresh coriander leaves, 1 tsp of cumin seeds, 1 tsp of sesame seeds, and salt to taste. Mix well.
  3. Knead the mixture into a firm dough by adding water gradually:
    Add water little by little and knead the mixture into a smooth dough. The dough should be firm, but not too hard or too soft.
  4. Divide the dough into small portions and flatten them into disc shapes:
    Divide the dough into small portions and shape them into small balls. Take a ball of the dough and press it between your palms to flatten it into a disc shape.
  5. Cook the flattened dough on a heated pan or griddle, drizzle oil, and flip to cook both sides until golden brown:
    Heat a flat pan or griddle over medium heat. Place the flattened dough onto the heated pan and gently press it with your fingers to make it thin and even. Make 2-3 small holes in the center of the dough to ensure even cooking. Drizzle a little oil on top of the dough and around the edges. Cook the sarva pindi on medium heat for 4-5 minutes on one side or until it turns golden brown. Flip the sarva pindi and cook it on the other side for another 4-5 minutes or until it turns golden brown.
  6. Repeat the process with remaining dough portions:
    Remove the cooked sarva pindi from the pan and transfer it to a plate. Repeat the process with the remaining dough portions.
  7. Serve hot with chutney or curry of your choice:
    Serve the hot and crispy sarva pindi with any chutney or curry of your choice. Enjoy!
